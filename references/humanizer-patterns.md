## Critical Patterns for Video Scripts

These are the patterns that matter most for spoken-word content. A script that
reads "AI" on paper sounds even worse when read aloud on camera.

### 1. Inflated Significance

**Kill words**: stands as, serves as, testament, reminder, vital/significant/crucial/
pivotal/key role, underscores, highlights importance, reflects broader, symbolizing,
enduring, lasting, setting the stage, marking/shaping, represents a shift, indelible mark

**Fix**: State the fact plainly. Let the viewer decide if it's significant.

Before: "This peptide serves as a groundbreaking testament to modern longevity science."
After: "This peptide increased my deep sleep by 40 minutes. Here's the data."

### 2. Superficial -ing Analyses

**Kill pattern**: Any sentence ending with "...highlighting X", "...showcasing Y",
"...ensuring Z", "...reflecting W", "...emphasizing Q"

**Fix**: Make it a new sentence with a specific claim.

Before: "The formula combines three key ingredients, showcasing the brand's commitment
to science-backed skincare."
After: "The formula has three active ingredients. Each one has at least two clinical
trials behind it."

### 3. Promotional Language

**Kill words**: boasts, vibrant, rich (figurative), profound, enhancing, exemplifies,
commitment to, natural beauty, nestled, groundbreaking, renowned, breathtaking,
must-try, stunning, game-changer, revolutionary

**Fix**: Replace with specific, measurable claims.

Before: "This stunning skincare line boasts a revolutionary approach to anti-aging."
After: "In 12 weeks, 87% of users saw visible wrinkle reduction. I was one of them."

### 4. AI Vocabulary (overused words)

**Avoid**: Additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance,
fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key (adj),
landscape (abstract), pivotal, showcase, tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore,
valuable, vibrant, leverage, innovative, seamless, robust, holistic, synergy

**Replace with**: also, match, important, explore, stress, lasting, improve,
encourage, get, show, connection, complex, main, area, important, show, mix,
proof, stress, useful, lively, use, new, smooth, strong, complete, teamwork

### 5. Negative Parallelisms

**Kill pattern**: "Not only X, but also Y" / "It's not just X, it's Y" /
"It's not merely X, it's Y"

**Fix**: Just say the second part. If both parts matter, make them two sentences.

Before: "It's not just a supplement, it's a lifestyle revolution."
After: "I take it every morning. It changed how I sleep, how I focus, how I train."

### 6. Rule of Three Overuse

**Kill pattern**: Groups of exactly three ("X, Y, and Z") appearing multiple times.

**Fix**: Vary groupings. Use two sometimes. Use four. Break the rhythm.

Before: "Improved energy, better focus, and deeper sleep."
After: "My energy is up. My focus is sharper. And honestly? The sleep improvement
surprised me the most."

### 7. Em Dash Overuse

**Fix**: Replace with commas, periods, or "and". One em dash per script max.

### 8. Copula Avoidance

**Kill pattern**: "serves as" / "stands as" / "marks" / "represents" / "boasts" / "features"
when "is" or "has" works fine.

Before: "This product serves as the cornerstone of our wellness line."
After: "This is our best-selling product."

### 9. Generic Positive Conclusions

**Kill pattern**: "The future looks bright" / "Exciting times ahead" / "continues to
thrive" / "journey toward excellence"

**Fix**: End with a specific CTA or concrete next step.

Before: "The future of skincare has never looked brighter."
After: "Link in bio. Use code GLOW20 for 20% off your first order."

## Voice-Specific Adjustments

After removing AI patterns, adjust for the brand voice:

| Voice Setting | What to Add |
|---------------|-------------|
| Professional | Measured confidence, data citations, industry terminology |
| Casual-fun | Contractions, slang, rhetorical questions, "honestly/tbh" |
| Edgy-bold | Short sentences, strong opinions, challenges to viewer |
| Scientific | Precise terminology, study references, mechanism explanations |
| Spiritual-wellness | Sensory language, personal journey framing, intention-setting |

## Quick Checklist (run on every script)

- [ ] No "serves as" / "stands as" / "testament"
- [ ] No -ing analysis phrases at end of sentences
- [ ] No promotional superlatives without evidence
- [ ] No AI vocabulary words (check list above)
- [ ] No "not only X, but Y" constructions
- [ ] Rule of three broken at least once
- [ ] Em dashes: max 1 per script
- [ ] Sentences vary in length (some 3 words, some 20)
- [ ] At least one specific number, date, or name
- [ ] CTA is concrete, not aspirational
- [ ] Reads naturally when spoken aloud at 2.5 words/second
